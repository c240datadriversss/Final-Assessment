EGG SUBSTITUTION GUIDE FOR BAKING
(Binding, Moisture, & Leavening)

==================================================
OVERVIEW
==================================================

Eggs serve different purposes in baking such as binding, moisture, richness,
and leavening. The best substitute depends on what role the egg plays in your
recipe. Below are common egg substitutes grouped by function.

==================================================
FOR BINDING & MOISTURE
(Cookies, Muffins, Quick Breads)
==================================================

Flax or Chia Egg:
- Mix 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed or chia seeds
  with 3 tablespoons water
- Let sit 5–10 minutes until gelatinous
- Replaces 1 egg
- Best for cookies, muffins, pancakes

Mashed Banana:
- 1/4 cup mashed ripe banana per egg
- Adds moisture and sweetness
- Best for brownies, muffins, quick breads

Applesauce:
- 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce per egg
- Adds moisture with mild flavor
- Best for cakes, muffins, brownies

Silken Tofu:
- 1/4 cup pureed silken tofu per egg
- Adds density and structure
- Best for pies, cheesecakes, dense cakes

Yogurt or Buttermilk:
- 1/4 cup plain yogurt or buttermilk per egg
- Reduce other liquid by 1/4 cup
- Adds richness and tenderness

==================================================
FOR LEAVENING & LIGHTNESS
(Cakes, Pancakes, Cupcakes)
==================================================

Vinegar & Baking Soda:
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 tablespoon white or apple cider vinegar
- Creates lift and airiness
- Best for cakes and cupcakes

Water, Oil & Baking Powder:
- 2 tablespoons water
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1 teaspoon oil
- Best for cookies and quick breads

Carbonated Water:
- Use in place of liquid in recipe
- Adds lightness
- Best for pancakes and light cakes

==================================================
FOR EGG WHITES & FOAMING
==================================================

Aquafaba (Chickpea Liquid):
- 3 tablespoons aquafaba = 1 egg
- Whips like egg whites
- Best for meringues, macarons, light cakes

==================================================
OTHER EGG SUBSTITUTES
==================================================

Commercial Egg Replacer:
- Follow package directions
- Neutral flavor
- Reliable for most baked goods

Nut Butter:
- 3 tablespoons per egg
- Adds richness and density
- Best for brownies and dense cookies

==================================================
TIPS FOR SUCCESS
==================================================

- Identify the egg’s role: binding, moisture, lift, or richness
- Expect flavor changes with banana, flax, or nut butter
- Most substitutes replace one egg; adjust liquids if needed
- Dense bakes tolerate substitutes better than airy cakes
