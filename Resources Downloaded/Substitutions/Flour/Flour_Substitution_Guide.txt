FLOUR SUBSTITUTION GUIDE FOR BAKING
(Structure, Texture, & Gluten-Free Options)
==================================================
OVERVIEW
==================================================
Flour provides structure and texture in baked goods. Substitutes vary in protein, absorbency, and flavor. Below are common flour alternatives with ratios and tips.

==================================================
COMMON FLOUR SUBSTITUTES
==================================================
Whole Wheat Flour:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Denser texture; add 1â€“2 tbsp extra liquid
- Best for: Cookies, muffins, quick breads

Oat Flour:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Chewy texture; slightly nutty flavor
- Best for: Cookies, bars, pancakes

Almond Flour:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Gluten-free; add binding agent (egg or xanthan gum)
- Best for: Cookies, brownies, dense cakes

Coconut Flour:
- Ratio: 1/4 cup coconut flour per 1 cup all-purpose
- Notes: Highly absorbent; increase liquid and eggs
- Best for: Cookies, bars, gluten-free recipes

Rice Flour:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Gluten-free; lighter texture
- Best for: Shortbread, crisp cookies

Chickpea Flour:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Adds protein; earthy taste
- Best for: Savory cookies, crackers

Spelt Flour:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Mild nutty flavor; slightly less gluten
- Best for: Cookies, muffins, breads

Gluten-Free Blend:
- Ratio: 1:1 for all-purpose flour
- Notes: Use blends with xanthan gum for structure
- Best for: Most baked goods

==================================================
TIPS FOR SUCCESS
==================================================
- Adjust liquids when using absorbent flours (coconut, oat)
- Add binders for gluten-free flours (egg, flax, xanthan gum)
- Expect flavor changes with nut-based flours
- Test small batches for texture and spread
