Sugars ğŸ¬

Replace: 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar - 1:1 replacement, adds caramel flavor and moisture, creates slightly denser texture
  • 1 cup coconut sugar - 1:1 replacement, similar sweetness intensity, lower glycemic index, nutty caramel notes
  • 1 cup date sugar - 1:1 replacement, nutrient-rich and very sweet, may dry baked goods slightly, adjust moisture as needed
  • 3/4 cup honey - Natural sweetener with vitamins and antioxidants, reduce other liquids by 1/4 cup, adds moisture and golden color
  • 1 cup maple syrup - Natural sugar with minerals, reduce other liquids by 1/4 cup, adds subtle maple flavor and moisture
  • 1 cup agave nectar - Milder sweetness than honey, reduce other liquids by 1/3 cup, natural sugar alternative
  • 1 cup stevia baking blend - Plant-based, zero calories, 1:1 replacement with blends, add moisture ingredients like banana or yogurt, may have slight aftertaste
  • 1/2 cup monk fruit sweetener - Zero calories, very sweet (use half the amount), may affect browning, derived from monk fruit extract
  • 1 cup erythritol - Sugar alcohol with 70% sweetness of sugar, very low calories, doesn't spike blood sugar, may cause digestive upset in large amounts
  • 1 cup allulose - Novel sweetener with 70% sweetness of sugar, very low calories, found naturally in figs and maple syrup, bakes similarly to sugar

ğŸ’¡ Pro Tip: Liquid sweeteners (honey, maple syrup, agave) can affect your batter consistency, so adjust flour or liquid as needed. For low-calorie baking, choose stevia, monk fruit, or erythritol. Natural sugars like honey and maple syrup still count as added sugars and impact blood sugar.
